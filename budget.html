How we got here — the audit chain

2026-05-14 — the sheet looked done. Three-agent re-audit + TCW 2023 floor verification reconciled to $324,937 all-in at the $325K cap with $63 of cushion. Contingency was already reduced 11% → 5.4% as the lever to fit.
2026-05-15 morning — G1 deep-dive. A two-agent per-line itemization of Group 1 (Claude + Codex with citations) surfaced $48,150 of hidden cost in G1 alone. The single biggest surprise: hardwood refinish with Bleach + Rubio Pure (the locked non-negotiable spec) is a specialty premium process, not a standard refinish — honest cost $11,200, not $5,500. The sheet's "$63 under cap" was structural fiction.
2026-05-15 afternoon — full-sheet vet. Six agents (Codex + Gemini-3-pro + four specialized Claude subagents) extended the line-by-line method to G2 through G7. Total honest gap surfaced: $85,645 across the sheet, not just G1.

The HARD RULE that governed the revision

Revisions move UP, never DOWN. Once a line is at TCW 2023 × 1.10 floor or honest 2026 market, it cannot be value-engineered downward as a budget-fit lever. To fit a cap, only three moves are legal: (a) reduce contingency %, (b) defer entire scope items to Phase 2, (c) revise the cap upward. Trimming a line item's dollar figure to make math work is exactly the kind of self-deception the multi-agent process is designed to surface.
