The Japandi-Boho Spectrum

Phase 1: Philosophy Lock · 1490 Lively Ridge · 67 references audited across 7 positions

What you're looking at

Seven positions across the Japandi↔Boho spectrum, each shown with multiple reference rooms (3-15) so you can see the aesthetic as a zone, not as a single photo. Three positions (1, 6, 7) are kill cases — included to define what we're rejecting. The other four (2, 3, 4, 5) are real candidates.

Heavy audit pass: 15 of the prior 33 images were removed for being off-aesthetic, exterior shots, palette violations, or wrong-position attributions. The remaining 67 each had to pass per-position signature checks: clear exemplar, not borderline.

How to react:

  1. Scroll through all 7 positions — each is a zone, not a single image. The variety inside is the point.
  2. Identify the position closest to where you instinctively want to land, and the adjacent position you also like.
  3. Tell me the two adjacent positions. Example: "between 3 and 4, leaning 3" → we lock warm-Japandi-with-light-boho.
  4. Bonus: name a specific image you feel strongest about (e.g., "P3 — 3m specifically, the Azabu boucle"). That tells me about palette warmth, formality, room-type preferences inside the locked zone.

Pure Japandi Minimal

5 refs Kill Case · Too Sparse

Monk-quiet, near-empty, one painting on a wall, single objects load-bearing, dark or pale timber, zero textile layering, zero color. Beautiful, but reads as a meditation hall, not a place to live.

5

Boho-Leaning Neutral

12 refs

Heavy textile stacking, vintage layering, cognac leather or printed sofa, antique rug as foundation, fiddle leaf tree, stacked books in every alcove. Palette still earth-toned (no jewel saturation, no macramé) but visual density tripled vs P3. This is where Japandi is overrun by a confident layered-vintage hand. Curator's flag: picking 5 over 4 means crossing into a meaningfully different aesthetic.
