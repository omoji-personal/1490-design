1. The aesthetic in one sentence

A 1953 Atlanta brick ranch executed as California Modern Japandi, translated honestly for Southern compressed-ceiling architecture: light + warm + restrained + lived-in, with Japandi discipline (restraint, palette, wabi-sabi) running underneath and modern California domestic warmth on top.

2. The aesthetic name (final)

"California Modern Japandi — Atlanta Translation"

The "Atlanta Translation" qualifier is intentional. The canon designers work in vaulted-California-loft conditions; we work in 8-ft-ceiling Southern ranch conditions. Our execution will look like kin of the canon, not clones.

3a. The 9-element CALIFORNIA MODERN JAPANDI composite signature (from image audit)

Across the 18 sourceable anchors, these 9 visual elements appear in >50% of clear-canon frames. This is the operational definition for the renovation:

  1. Light oak wide-plank floor (17 of 18 anchors)
  2. Plaster, cream, or soft-white walls — never saturated, never wallpapered, never beadboard/shiplap in public rooms
  3. One warm-wood gesture per surface — oak island OR oak built-in OR oak coffee table; not all three at once
  4. Marble (Carrara, Calacatta, or quartz-look) for kitchen counters/backsplash — bookmatched or slab; not subway tile
  5. Brass hardware + at least one brass light fixture per public room — globe pendant, branching chandelier, or disc sconce. Never gold-gold; always brushed/aged brass
  6. One cream linen upholstered anchor per LR (sectional or sofa). Never patterned, never tufted
  7. One real plant tree at floor height per public room — fiddle leaf fig is the canon default. Cat-safe substitution mandate: spec parlor palm OR kentia palm OR bird of paradise (all cat-safe, same vertical-leaf-tree presence)
  8. One "real color" accent per room — sage olive, mustard, rust, or olive-glass green. Never more than one accent color per room; never primary colors; never jewel tones
  9. Hand-thrown ceramics in restrained groupings (3-5 per shelf), matte-clay or muted-glaze, never matched commercial dinnerware

Secondary signals (in 30-50% of anchors)

  • Nelson Bubble Saucer or generic paper-globe pendant
  • Wishbone CH24 or paper-cord oak chair silhouette in dining
  • Beni Ourain or neutral wool rug (deferred to year 4)
  • Sheer cream linen Roman shades or drapes
  • Hans Wegner / Aalto / mid-century light-wood furniture forms

Anti-signals — MUST NOT APPEAR (final reconciled kill list)

  • Dark-stained or dark walnut floors
  • Beadboard, shiplap, board-and-batten walls in public rooms (cali_25 has subtle plank walls in a bedroom only — borderline edge case)
  • Walnut as PRIMARY cabinetry (accent permitted)
  • Open shelves carrying matched-set commercial dishware
  • Saturated wall paint (terracotta, navy, emerald) — single-accent textile is the only color delivery
  • Gilt mirrors, antlers, taxidermy, William Morris wallpaper, dreamcatchers, macramé walls
  • Chrome, polished nickel, exposed Edison cages, crystal, dark bronze
  • Glass coffee tables on chrome legs, lucite, mirrored furniture
  • Espresso / mahogany / cherry / pickled-whitewash wood finishes
  • High-saturation kilim revival rugs, pattern-on-pattern textile stacking
  • Shiplap with barn doors with black metal grid ("modern farmhouse")

5e. Brass vs bright-gold distinction (owner clarified 2026-05-16)

The canon's "warm brass + matte black mix" includes the lacquered, unlacquered, antique, and aged-brass family, plus Champagne Bronze (plumbing, Delta Trinsic) — all warm, restrained, matte-leaning gold tones.

EXCLUDED from the canon — do not specify or select: - Bright gold ("Luxe Gold," Brizo Litze -GL suffix) - Polished gold (mirror-finish, hotel-bathroom register) - "Brushed Gold" when the product reads shiny/modern rather than warm-matte - PVD gold / champagne gold variants that read lighter/brighter than Champagne Bronze - Any bare "gold" finish without a "bronze" or "brass" qualifier, when the finish looks reflective rather than warm-matte

Practical filter rule: If a fixture looks like it belongs in a hotel-luxury bathroom or a modern minimalist showroom, it is too shiny. Stay in the warm-brass family.

Canonical warm brass examples (these are fine): - Lacquered brass (Schoolhouse, Rejuvenation, Cedar & Moss) - Unlacquered / natural / antique / aged brass (Emtek, Hamilton Sinkler, Visual Comfort HARAB) - Champagne Bronze — Delta Trinsic CZ family (plumbing, locked canon) - Vibrant Brushed Bronze — Kohler BV family (warm bronze variant, acceptable) - Brushed Brass — Sonoma Forge and similar (warm matte brass, acceptable for outdoor/accent)

Non-canonical bright gold examples (reject on sight): - Brizo Litze Luxe Gold (-GL SKU suffix) - Any faucet/fixture described as "Brushed Gold" by Brizo, WINGSOL, or similar modern faucet brands - "Champagne Gold" (lighter and brighter than Champagne Bronze — distinct and excluded) - Loqed or other hardware in generic "Gold finish" (shiny gold, not warm brass)

This clarification supersedes any prior option reasoning that described Luxe Gold or Brushed Gold finishes as premium upgrades or acceptable alternate finish families. Those options remain in sourcing.yaml for reference but are tagged as owner-rejected.

6a. Gold/brass weight calibration (2026-05-16 owner feedback)

Prior canon described "brass + matte black mix" but the sourcing tracker defaulted brass-first on most lighting and hardware ★ picks. Owner feedback 2026-05-16: "this is using too much gold overall." Calibration rule:

  • Matte black is the DEFAULT for utility tier — basement bath cabinet hardware, basement bath sconces, hall bath sconces, mudroom lighting, mudroom hooks (when not in mixed-finish mode), back-deck-door hardware, exterior lighting (already locked black per §8d Atlanta humidity), interior cabinet pulls in secondary rooms.
  • Matte black is tied to FUNCTIONAL ZONES inside finish-locked rooms — example: in any bath, the towel BARS stay Champagne Bronze (consistent with the Trinsic plumbing finish-family lock — see §6 plumbing row) but the HOOKS, robe hooks, and accessory pulls can shift to matte black to enforce the brass+black mix without violating the plumbing lock.
  • Brass remains the ANCHOR finish for hero items — kitchen pendants (when active), dining chandelier, master bedroom ceiling + sconces, master bath sconces (Princeton family), front-door hardware, plumbing trim throughout (Champagne Bronze LOCKED), kitchen + master cabinet pulls (brass-led in mixed-finish K-CAB).
  • Bath mirrors default to bleached / natural wood frames in secondary baths — the brass-framed mirror moment is reserved for places where the wall needs the metal anchor (LR / entry), not stacked on top of brass cabinet pulls + brass plumbing trim in a small bath.
  • Target finish ratio across ★ recommends in finish-relevant categories (lighting + hardware + plumbing): roughly 50% brass, 35% matte black, 15% other (silver / wood / painted / Trinsic-locked-counts-as-brass). Audit-baseline pre-calibration was 51% brass / 8% black — per 2026-05-16 brass-rebalance pass: 7 utility-tier items shifted brass→matte black (HB-SCONCES, BB-SCONCES, MUDROOM-LIGHTING, LR-FLOOR-LAMP, BACK-DECK-DOOR, TOWEL-BARS-HOOKS, BATH-MIRRORS) plus 4 items got matte-black alternates added (DR-PICTURE-LIGHT, HB-CAB-HARDWARE, MBA-SCONCES, OFFICE-LIGHTING).
  • Items that did NOT shift: all plumbing (Trinsic Champagne Bronze LOCKED per §6 row), DR-CHANDELIER (dining brass-anchor hero), MB-CEILING + MB-SCONCES (master BR brass-anchor hero), LR-SCONCES + LR-MIRROR (LR brass anchors — paired with shifted LR-FLOOR-LAMP black for the room-level mix), FRONT-DOOR (entry brass hero), K-CAB-HARDWARE + MUDROOM-HOOKS (already explicit brass+black mix), INTERIOR-DOOR-HARDWARE (already decided matte black for all 10 doors).

8. Atlanta-context translation rules (NEW SECTION — was missing)

The audit's biggest finding: every canon reference is California vaulted-loft. Our house is 1953 Atlanta brick ranch with 8-ft ceilings + heavy tree canopy + 90% summer humidity. Honest translation rules:

8a. Ceiling-height-specific lighting

  • Pendants <16" in major-traffic areas (don't drop a Nelson Saucer Bubble that's beautiful at 9' ceiling into 8' room — it becomes an obstacle).
  • Sconces should carry more lighting load than pendants in most rooms (sconce + recessed combo).
  • Recessed lighting plan required to supplement decorative — 2700K gimbaled (not 3000K which reads cool) for cloudy/tree-shaded Atlanta days.

8b. Compressed-room volume

  • Drop "open plan" expectations — your house has structural-brick walls, not movable partitions. The aesthetic must work in well-resolved compressed rooms.
  • Density target stays 5-7 — pushing to 7-9 with 8-ft ceilings reads as cluttered.
  • Vertical proportions matter more than canon photos suggest — tall thin floor lamps, vertical art, tall narrow plants help fight ceiling compression.

8c. Light strategy

  • Maximum-window-glass moves (clerestory, gable-window, sliding-glass-wall) are not in budget. Re-baseline expectations: "light + bright California" will not photograph the same in Atlanta. Plan for warm not bright.
  • Sheer linen drapery + light-color upholstery preserve what daylight does reach the rooms.
  • Reflective surfaces (Carrara marble, brass mirror surrounds, sheer linen) bounce limited light.

8d. Humidity protocol

  • HVAC humidity control to 35-55% RH — non-negotiable for the spec. Without it: Rubio floors gap/cup, brass tarnishes faster, plaster walls show movement, linen mildews.
  • Wood specifications: rift-sawn or quarter-sawn for cabinet doors and built-ins (more stable than plain-sawn against humidity swings).
  • Bath ventilation: humidistat-controlled exhaust fans (already in scope) — non-optional given Atlanta summer.

8e. Regional appropriateness

  • Brick exterior stays — and the design should acknowledge it. A Bay Area Eichler aesthetic inside Southern brick-rancher bones is a tension that can be elegant (call it intentional translation) or jarring (looks like you ignored the house).
  • Landscape brief (Section 11) needs Atlanta-appropriate plant palette, not California native.
