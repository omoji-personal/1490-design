Cathie Hong Interiors

The anchor designer. Campbell House (Rue Magazine) is THE spine project — kitchen, LR, dining, bedroom all canonical.

The anchor. Cathie Hong Interiors is the spine of 1490's canon. Campbell House (Rue Magazine, 2023) is THE single reference that ties the whole direction together — kitchen, LR, dining, primary bedroom all land within the canon.

Why she anchors

  • Warmth without messiness. Light oak + cream + brass without veering boho or rustic. The exact temperature of "warm Japandi."
  • Repeatable formula. Multiple projects (Campbell House, plus 5+ others) hit the same 9-element signature — proves it's a language, not a one-off.
  • Color discipline. One real-color accent per room. Never more. Never primary. Never tufted.
  • California sensibility, transferable to Atlanta. Her work is climate-agnostic enough that the formula translates to a 1953 Atlanta brick ranch without feeling out-of-place.

What to steal directly

  • The kitchen cabinet color + counter + backsplash trinity
  • The "one warm-wood gesture per surface" discipline (the oak island IS the wood; the floor and built-in stay neutral)
  • The brass pendant density (3-4 per kitchen, not 1-2 — repetition creates rhythm)
  • The plant placement convention — kentia palm or bird of paradise (NOT fiddle leaf, which is toxic to cats)
  • The cream-linen-sofa decisiveness — no patterns, no tufting, just one anchor
  • The ceramic restraint — 3-5 pieces per surface, never more
Owner takeaway: If a decision feels unclear, ask "what would Cathie Hong do here?" She's the tie-breaker.
