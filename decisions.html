How we got here: 6+ rounds of mood-board iteration → tri-audit (Claude + Gemini + Codex) → 48-hour reconciliation sprint → 29 philosophical Q&A → 7 owner-decision locks. This page is the digestible summary. Full reasoning in the Design Spec.

The 7 Owner Decisions

1

Kitchen backsplash

Pivot

Originally locked: Cle Weathered White Bejmat counter-to-ceiling.

LOCKED: Pivot to Carrara slab on range wall + Cle Sea Salt zellige squares elsewhere. Keep Cle Bejmat in master bath shower walls only.

Image audit flagged Bejmat as visually inconsistent with the canon kitchens (every Cathie Hong + OWIU + Studio LIFE/STYLE kitchen uses Carrara/Calacatta slab + zellige, not Bejmat). The pivot aligns with our anchor projects without losing Cle as a high-impact bath moment.

2

Beni Ourain vintage rug

Defer

Source year 1 as the boho-softening LR rug, or defer to later?

LOCKED: Defer to year 4+. Spec WE/Rejuvenation flat-weave wool placeholder for years 1-3 (~$800-1.5K vs $3-5K vintage). Real Beni Ourain becomes the post-pet-mature / post-potty-train indulgence.

40-lb Labradoodle + cat + newborn ~1yr makes vintage wool a near-certain catastrophic stain risk. Owner picked "no patina romance" — buying a $3-5K vintage rug that will be permanently stained by year 3 doesn't fit.

3

Performance fabric pivot

Pivot

Pure Belgian flax linen or performance-fabric pivot for primary seating + drapery?

LOCKED: Pivot primary sofa + drapery to performance linen (Crypton Linen, Perennials, or similar). Pure flax retained only for master bedding (laundered weekly anyway).

Drops photographic look ~5%, drops maintenance panic ~80%. With curated-visibility household priority + heavy hosting + pets + baby, performance fabric is the rational choice. Owner's no-patina-romance reinforces the call.

4

Brass finish

Pivot to Lacquered

Unlacquered brass (patinates over time) or lacquered brass (stays shiny)?

LOCKED: Lacquered brass throughout — plumbing, cabinet hardware, light fixtures. Stays shiny indefinitely.

Owner explicitly rejected patina romance (Q3 of philosophy calibration: "no I don't particularly see value in that"). Lacquered brass eliminates annual Renaissance Wax maintenance cycle and Atlanta humidity blotchy-tarnish failure mode. Lower wabi-sabi credit but matches owner's "stays looking good" priority.

5

Furniture envelope

$30K Cap

$30K vs $80-100K phased over 3-4 years for "mostly settled" day 1?

LOCKED: $30K total cap. Focus on upstairs primary rooms. Reuse existing furniture in basement / overflow zones.

Owner has a hard ceiling at $30K in addition to renovation. Strategic allocation: LR ($10-12K) + Dining ($4-6K) + Master BR ($5-6K) + Office ($3-4K) + Outdoor deck ($5-7K). Day-1 "mostly settled" lands on upstairs only; basement looks functional-not-finished. Acceptable trade.

6

Front door

Walnut

Walnut pivot, walnut standard swing, or keep existing?

LOCKED (designer commit, no owner pushback): Walnut natural-stain standard swing door (~$1-2K premium over standard prehung).

Walnut entry is the canon Campbell House move (Cathie Hong Campbell House entry pivot reference — owner-loved). Standard swing keeps the budget reasonable vs a pivot mechanism. Walnut now permitted as accent per reconciled rules.

7

Custom millwork sub-budget

$25-35K

How much custom millwork investment within the renovation construction budget?

LOCKED: $25-35K custom millwork within the $342K cap (construction subtotal $312,398). Scope:

  • Kitchen oak island base + light interior built-ins ($8-12K)
  • Architected freestanding bar shelf wall (street-facing kitchen wall — solves bar + storage + console in one move) ($6-10K)
  • Office built-in desk wall (light Wellington-Office style) ($5-8K)
  • Mudroom built-in bench + hooks + storage ($2-4K)
  • LR TV/storage cabinetry (1 wall) ($4-6K)

Canon look depends on custom millwork; can't get there with pure retail furniture at $30K. Owner can recheck the construction budget for headroom; if not available, aesthetic shifts toward retail-furniture-heavy.

The 5 Philosophical Calibrations

How the design feels, not what to buy

1. Walking-in feeling
Warm + welcomed (priority over sanctuary, calm, focus)
2. Light strategy
Maximize light — tree-canopied lot has low ambient; design works AGAINST that with reflective surfaces, sheer drapery, recessed cans, light-color palette, NO moody-evening rooms
3. Patina philosophy
Owner rejected. Materials should stay looking good. Lacquered brass over unlacquered; durable plaster over limewash variation; Rubio floors stay (durable hardwax oil, not romantic patina language). Leather still fine (softens user-friendly).
4. Indoor/outdoor relationship
Like visual seamlessness (big windows, sliding glass) but don't actually live outdoors much in humid summer / cold winter. Outdoor = pleasant feature, not lifestyle anchor.
5. Rules vs permission
Selective breaks permitted. When an object brings real joy, rules become guidelines. Kill-list still firm (no saturated walls, no dark walnut primary, no jewel tones, no Modern Farmhouse).

How the household uses the house

6. Hosting rhythm
Mixed + IMPORTANT. Weekly drinks + dinner gatherings. Monthly bigger parties up to 20 people. Omid makes craft cocktails. Bar zone = architected freestanding shelf on street-facing kitchen wall.
7. Universal vs uniquely "us"
Uniquely us. House should tell YOUR family's story, not magazine-elegant-anonymous.
8. "Real life" visibility
Curated. Some functional items live in the open (designed dog bed, charging station); other stuff hidden (TV in Frame mode, cables, baby toys, recycling).
9. Iranian heritage integration
Limited. One Persian rug somewhere + maybe one Iranian-modern art piece. Not a dominant theme.
10. Annika's voice
Aligned with direction. No separate Annika-specific design process needed.
11. Day rhythm
Mornings: front or back deck when weather is nice; otherwise office. Skip breakfast usually. Evenings: kitchen + LR couch (cooking, drinking, TV, catching up).
16. Music infrastructure
HUGE. Sony floor-standing speakers + amplifier + HUE lights everywhere. Speakers + amp live by the TV; couch is the listening sweet-spot. No vinyl. Loud, high-quality, not gimmicky.
22. TV
Samsung Frame TV wall-mounted (existing, may upgrade size). Disappears as art when off.
14. Color direction
Earthy warm preferred: rust, mustard, deep olive, oxblood, terracotta. Less cool-leaning warm (sage / petrol used selectively, not dominantly).
15. Spatial preference
Maximize open feeling — already enforced in kitchen/LR/dining area. Don't add walls back.
23. Office (WFH)
Heavy Zoom. 1 monitor + laptop monitor. Mostly work, secondary library if room. No privacy concern.
27. Plant care
Full commitment. 3-5 per social room, 1-2 private. Cat-safe defaults (parlor palm not fiddle leaf in cat zones).
28. Nursery timing
TBD. Design framework only, defer real sourcing.
29. Outdoor deck
Moderate. Outdoor dining for 4 + 2 lounge chairs (~$5-7K within the $30K furniture envelope).
