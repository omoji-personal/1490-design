# Rejected Aesthetics — Reference Manifest

Anti-pattern references for the 1490 Lively Ridge /rejected page. The owners
chose California Modern Japandi (warm + light + lived-in). Each image below
shows a designer/style they EXPLICITLY don't want, with the visual element
that puts it on the kill list.

rejected_01.jpg — Norm Architects, Copenhagen apartment (Ignant, 2015) — REJECTED: too cold/austere, hard-edged minimalism with no warmth or coziness; reads clinical rather than lived-in.
rejected_02.jpg — Norm Architects, monochrome interior (Ignant, 2015) — REJECTED: greyscale palette and pared-back severity; the "soft minimalism" theory but missing the warm-Japandi heart we want.
rejected_03.jpg — Daytrip Studio, Queen's Park House kitchen (Elle Decoration UK) — REJECTED: dark fumed-oak floors + London-modern walnut joinery; the moody dark-stained wood that kills our light-and-airy California target.
rejected_04.jpg — Athena Calderone, Cobble Hill family room (EyeSwoon) — REJECTED: navy-plaster walls + ceiling, classic Brooklyn-dark; closes the room down where we want it to open up.
rejected_05.jpg — Athena Calderone, Brooklyn family room jewel-tone vignette (EyeSwoon) — REJECTED: velvet jewel-tones against deep saturated walls; too formal/library-dark for a warm Japandi home.
rejected_06.jpg — Heidi Caillier, Ballard Cottage dining room — REJECTED: cottage-traditional throughout — shiplap, wainscoting, traditional trim and millwork; we've moved past the cottage vocabulary (Inverness was the last gasp).
rejected_07.jpg — Reath Design, Holmby House play room (Remodelista) — REJECTED: saturated jewel-tone velvet sectional + bold pattern-on-pattern; the "rich moody color" Reath signature directly conflicts with our light-warm-Japandi palette.
rejected_08.jpg — Justina Blakeney / Jungalow, Melrose pop-up styling — REJECTED: Coachella maximalist boho — pattern stacking, macramé, kilim revival, plant overload; the opposite of restrained Japandi.
